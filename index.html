Project 1

VirtualCare App

Improving Healthcare Access During COVID-19

VirtualCare: The Challenge

During COVID-19, patients at Weiss Memorial Hospital struggled to access outpatient care through the digital platform.

1

Hidden Availability

Patients spent 2+ hours calling unavailable clinics

2

Communication Gaps

No async options, forced into phone tag

3

Scheduling Confusion

Unclear confirmations led to missed appointments

?

Research Needed

How do we fix these barriers?

It's frustrating to spend hours calling providers, only to find out they aren't accepting new patients. I just gave up.

VirtualCare: Research Approach

Discovery Research

  • 10 semi-structured interviews (45-60 min)
  • Recruited via hospital outpatient program
  • Thematic analysis with affinity mapping
  • Validated with hospital call data

Usability Testing

  • Round 1: 10 participants, 60% success
  • Identified 3 critical UI issues
  • Round 2: 10 participants, 85% success
  • Validated design improvements

Key Metrics Tracked

  • Task completion rate
  • Time on task
  • Error rate
  • User satisfaction (1-5 scale)
  • SUS score

Project 2

NISRA Website Redesign

Web Accessibility & Information Architecture

NISRA: The Challenge

NISRA's website failed basic accessibility standards, preventing visually impaired users from signing up independently.

1

Small Text

Users couldn't read program details comfortably

2

Poor Contrast

Failed WCAG 2.0 AA standards

3

Unclear Labels

Form fields confused users and screen readers

4

Navigation Issues

Cluttered menus, unclear structure

WCAG Contrast Audit Results

Body Text
3.2:1
Form Labels
2.8:1
Required (WCAG)
4.5:1
Forms feel like a guessing game. I don't know what to enter. The text was too small and the colors made it hard to read.

NISRA: Research Methodology

Competitive Analysis

  • Benchmarked against NWSRA and FVSRA
  • Identified 5 key accessibility gaps
  • NISRA lacked text resize and WCAG compliance

Contextual Inquiries

  • 10 participants with visual impairments
  • Observed via Zoom (real-time struggles)
  • 7 out of 10 couldn't read program details
  • 9 out of 10 hesitated at form fields

Card Sorting (2 Rounds)

  • Round 1: Identified confusion with categories
  • Round 2: 85% agreement on structure
  • 40% faster completion time

Tree Testing (2 Rounds)

  • Summer Camps: 74% to 91% success
  • Volunteer Info: 88% to 95% success
  • Event Registration: 79% to 88% success
  • Average time reduced by 23%

Usability Testing

  • 5 participants tested updated design
  • Tasks: resize text, read content, complete form
  • Identified 3 issues requiring iteration
  • Final validation showed 90% success

WCAG Audit Tool

WebAIM's Contrast Checker validated all findings and ensured compliance throughout iteration process.

NISRA: Accessibility Solutions

Research-Backed Improvements

Text Resize Controls

  • User-controlled text sizing button
  • Clear "Text Size" label added after testing
  • No browser zoom required

Color Contrast Updates

  • Achieved 4.5:1 ratio for body text
  • 3:1 ratio for large text (headers)
  • Fine-tuned with WebAIM until all passed

Clear Form Labels

  • Descriptive labels for all input fields
  • Screen reader compatible throughout
  • Visible validation messages

Information Architecture

  • Reduced categories from 8 to 5
  • Removed 15+ outdated pages
  • Clearer menu labels and structure

Key Research Learnings

⚗

Mixed Methods Matter

Combining qualitative insights with quantitative validation created undeniable cases for change.

🎯

Small Details, Big Impact

Moving a filter from hidden to visible increased task success by 20%. Never underestimate placement.

🔄

Iteration is Everything

First-round testing felt like failure, but treating it as learning led to dramatic improvements.

♿

Accessibility Drives Engagement

Meeting WCAG standards wasn't just ethical—it directly increased engagement by 20%.

What I'd Do Differently

  • Expand demographic diversity (include more 50+ users)
  • Conduct competitive analysis earlier in process
  • Create centralized research repository
  • Test provider workflows alongside patient flows
  • Implement A/B testing for direct comparison
  • Include accessibility experts for validation

Research Philosophy

The best solutions come from listening, observing, testing, and iterating—always with empathy for the people we're designing for. Research must center the voices of those we serve.
